Efficient HEPA

HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air Filter) means high-efficiency air filter in Chinese. HEPA is divided into five materials: PP (polypropylene) filter paper, glass fiber, composite PP/PET (polyethylene terephthalate, polyester) filter paper, melt blown polyester non-woven fabric, and melt blown glass fiber.

HEPA filter material is made of chemical fiber meltblown or fiber laminated or woven, and its filtration mechanism has the following four types:
(1) The first one is the interception mechanism, which works on large particles.
(2) The second mechanism is the influence of gravity. Particles with small volume and high density will experience a decrease in velocity when passing through a HEPA filter and naturally settle onto the HEPA. This process is somewhat similar to the sedimentation of sediment in water downstream of a river.
(3) The third mechanism of HEPA filters is the influence of airflow. Due to the uneven weaving of HEPA filters, a large number of air vortices are formed, and ultra small particles are adsorbed on the HEPA filter due to the influence of this cyclone, achieving the purpose of filtration.
(4) The fourth filtering mechanism is that ultrafine particles collide with HEPA filter fibers under Brownian motion and are affected by van der Waals forces, resulting in adsorption and filtration. It can be seen that in addition to the first mechanism of intercepting large particles, it utilizes the static electricity at the fiber roots to adsorb small particles and achieve filtration effect.
