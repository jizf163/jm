Artificial heart valve

Mechanical valve is a type of heart valve made of artificial materials that can be implanted into the heart to replace damaged natural heart valves, thereby achieving one-way blood flow and possessing the function of natural valves. The composition of a mechanical valve usually includes three parts: the hard material used to make the stent, the elastic material used to make the blocking body, and the textile material used to make the valve annulus. Bracket materials typically include cobalt chromium nickel alloy, titanium, polyacetal resin, and polytetrafluoroethylene.

Preparation of artificial blood vessels

Artificial blood vessels are usually made of synthetic materials such as nylon, Dacron, and expanded polytetrafluoroethylene (ePTFE), among which the market share of expanded polytetrafluoroethylene artificial blood vessels is relatively high. Artificial blood vessels are suitable for vascular bypass surgery throughout the body. Currently, artificial blood vessel products used in clinical practice include composite wall artificial blood vessels, autologous tissue graft artificial blood vessels, Y-shaped artificial blood vessels, cone shaped artificial blood vessels, etc.
