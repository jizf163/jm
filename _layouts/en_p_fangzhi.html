Generality

Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is formed by laminating PTFE microporous film with ordinary fabric using a special adhesive, which is not penetrated by water. However, sweat vapor emitted by the human body can diffuse or be transmitted to the outside through the fabric, without accumulating and condensing between the body surface and the fabric.

FUNCTIONAL FABRIC

Windproof performance

The mesh like multi-layer microporous structure of PTFE film has non transparent pore sizes. When wind contacts the surface of the film and encounters barriers, it will change direction or fold back, making it difficult for air to penetrate. Therefore, external airflow cannot directly contact the human body.

Thermal insulation performance

PTFE membrane has strong moisture permeability, which can timely transfer the steam discharged by the human body to the outside of the microporous membrane, prevent water vapor from condensing into liquid water, and reduce the heat conduction of water; At the same time, the external wind cannot penetrate, effectively preventing air convection, thus making the membrane have a good windproof and sand keeping effect. Tests have shown that in strong winds of 25m/s, the insulation effect of 4M-PTFE laminated fabric is 30-40% higher than that of traditional down jackets.

Waterproof performance

The water pressure resistance of Jiamo 4M-PTFE membrane can reach about 18000 mm water column (that is, it can be penetrated only when the pressure is greater than 18 m water column). The micropore diameter of the membrane is about 0.2 μ m, while the diameter of the drizzle is 400 μ m, the diameter of the heavy rain is about 3000 μ m, and the diameter of the rainstorm is more than 6000 μ m. Therefore, the micropore diameter of the membrane is 1/1000~1/20000 of the diameter of the water drop. At the same time, the PTFE surface energy is low, and the water is naturally hydrophobic, so the smallest raindrop cannot pass through the membrane micropore.

Moisture permeability performance

The international standard for testing moisture permeability performance is to use moisture permeability impedance as an indicator, which means that the smaller the impedance, the higher the air permeability. The detection value of the 4M-PTFE membrane composite fabric is 32603g/m2 · 24hr, with a moisture permeability of over 32 liters per day, which is more than 10 times that of commonly used fabrics.

Outdoor Fabric

PTFE film has excellent thermal stability and can be used normally from a minimum of -150 ℃ to a maximum of+260 ℃, while materials such as PU coatings or TPU composite fabrics will break and freeze at a temperature of -30 ℃. Japanese mountaineers wear PTFE membrane clothing and have climbed mountains up to 8000 meters high multiple times. The clothing has withstood the test of climate conditions of minus 30 ℃ and wind speeds of 30m/s, and the athletes have not suffered any frostbite. Jiamo 4M-PTFE membrane composite fabric can withstand high temperatures of 260 ℃ and low temperatures of -150 ℃, and can be used in harsh environments to provide protection. It is also an excellent preferred material for firefighting suits, polar mountaineering suits, and special environments.
