Hydrogen fuel cells for new energy vehicles

Proton exchange membrane fuel cell (PEMFC) is the most commonly used form of fuel cell, which has the advantages of small size, high working efficiency, rapid start-up, long lifespan, and high current. It is a power generation device that directly converts the chemical energy of hydrogen and oxygen into electrical energy. The basic principle is to supply hydrogen and oxygen to the anode and cathode respectively. Hydrogen diffuses outward through the anode and reacts with the electrolyte, releasing electrons through an external load to reach the cathode, thereby generating an electric current. According to statistics, PEMFC occupies an absolute dominant position in the shipment volume of fuel cell types, with both the number of units (currently over 44000 units per year) and megawatts (currently over 1000MW) maintaining high-speed growth over the past five years, with a market share of over 80%. The number of fuel cell vehicles in China in 2020 was 7355. According to the "Energy saving and New Energy Vehicle Technology Roadmap", the development goals for fuel cell vehicles by 2025 and 2030 are 165000 and 1 million respectively. It can be seen that proton exchange membranes, as the core components of fuel cells, have a very broad incremental market space in the next 3-5 years.
